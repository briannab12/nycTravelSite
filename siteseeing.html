See the best of NYC

There are so many amazing things to see in NYC. Some of the most famous buildings and landmarks are the Statue of Liberty, Empire State Building, One World Trade Center, Madison Square Garden, Rockefeller Center, and Time Square. NYC was home of the world’s tallest building for many years. There are multiple sightseeing tours available by bus, boat, and helicopter, but you don’t need to take a tour to see everything. Many of these sights can’t be missed if you are traveling around NYC, so keep your eyes peeled. Here is a list of places you must see before you leave NYC.

1. Central Park

Central Park is an urban park in the middle of NYC. It was designated a National Historic Landmark. Central Park offers carriage horse tours, restaurants, running races, a carousel, and free classical music concerts.

2. Empire State Building

The Empire State Building was the world's tallest building for 40 years. It is currently the fifth-tallest building in the United States. It was designated a national landmark.

3. Statue of Liberty

The Statue of Liberty is on Liberty Island. It was a gift from France to the United States in 1886. The statue symbolizes freedom and has greeted many immigrants as they came to NYC.

4. Rockefeller Center

Rockefeller Center is a large complex that is famous for its Christmas tree lighting. It was designated a National Historic Landmark.

5. Grand Central Terminal

Grand Central Terminal is known for its spectacular main concourse. The terminal is a large rapid transit railroad terminal in Manhattan.

6. Radio City Music Hall

Radio City Music Hall is an entertainment venue and is in Rockefeller Center. It is famous for being the home of the Rockettes.

7. Chinatown

Chinatown in Manhattan was given its name because of its high ethnic concentrating of Chinese people. It is the largest concentration of Chinese people in the Western Hemisphere. There are many great restaurants and shops to visit.

8. Little Italy

Little Italy in Manhattan was given its name because of its high ethnic concentrating of Itanian people. Many important members of the Italian Mafia lived in Little Italy. There are only a few restaurants and shops left to visit.

9. Madison Square Garden

Madison Square Garden is an indoor arena that is used for professional basketball, professional hockey, concerts, and other entertainment. Elton John, Frank Sentara, U2, Jethro Tull, Metallica, John Lennon, Guns N’ Roses, Grateful Dead, Green Day, Queen, Led Zeppelin, Elvis Presley, Whitney Houston, Bon Jovi, Billy Joel, Paul McCartney, Michael Jackson, Rolling Stones, AC/DC, Madonna, and George Harrison have all performed at Madison Square Garden.

10. One World Trade Center

One World Trade Center is a skyscraper that was built on Ground Zero. It is ranked the tallest building in the Western Hemisphere.

11. Brooklyn Bridge

The Brooklyn Bridge is a famous bridge that connects Manhattan and Brooklyn. The bridge was the world’s first steel-wire suspension bridge.

12. Yankee Stadium

This Yankee Stadium is home of the New York Yankees, a Major-League Baseball team. This stadium replaced the original Yankee Stadium in 2009. The original stadium was built in 1923.

13. Staten Island Ferry

The Staten Island Ferry is a passenger ferry that travels between Manhattan and Staten Island. You can get a great view of the Statue of Liberty while on the ferry ride.

14. Time Square

Time Square is famous for being the location of the News Year’s Eve ball drop. The tradition began in 1907. Time Square looks amazing at night because of all of the lights.

15. Chrysler Building

The Chrysler Building is a famous piece of architecture. It was once the world’s tallest building, until it was beat by the Empire State Building.
