Some of the greatest museums in the world are in NYC. NYC is the home of 83 museums including the Brooklyn Museum, the Museum of Modern Art, the Metropolitan Museum of Art, the Intrepid Sea, Air and Space Museum, the 9/11 Memorial Preview Site, the Museum of the City of New York, the New York Transit Museum, the Children's Museum Of Manhattan, and Ripley's Believe it or Not! Times Square. The Metropolitan Museum of Art is the largest art museum in the US and the Museum of Modern Art is recognized as one of the largest museums of modern art in the world. There are museums of art, culture, plants and animals, American history, world history, space, military equipment, literature, and more. There are hours of art to appreciate, years of culture to learn about, and fun for all ages and interest. Find the museum right for you. Here is a summary of some of the most interesting museums in NYC.

1. Museum of Natural History

The Museum of Natural History is a great place to learn about humans, plants, and animals. It is one of the biggest museums in the world. You could spend the whole day walking through just one section of the museum. One of the most interesting segments is dedicated to space with a meteor on display and a planetarium.

2. Metropolitan Museum of Art

The Metropolitan Museum of Art, also known as the Met, contains art from Egypt, Europe, America, Africa, Asia, India, Islam, and other countries. It houses drawings, painting, instruments, photographs, sculptures, carvings, and everything in-between. The Met also displays modern art, gardens, and libraries. There is something for everyone to enjoy.

3.Museum of Sex

The Museum of Sex is a great place to learn about the sex, past and current. The museum takes you through the history of condoms, sex toys, pornographic literature and pornographic video. It also displays sex inspired art pieces. The museum is tastefully done, thoroughly educational, and definitely entertaining.

4. The Museum of Modern Art (MoMA)

The MoMA displays a large collection of modern and contemporary art. There are drawing, painting, sculpture, photography, videos, and more on display. This is a great place to be inspired and discuss art with like-minded art lovers.

5. Intrepid Sea, Air and Space Museum

The Intrepid Sea, Air and Space Museum is a military museum at Pier 86. Some of the popular exhibits include an aircraft career, a submarine, a space shuttle, and a supersonic reconnaissance plane. This is the only museum in NY displaying a spacecraft.
